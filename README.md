# Digital-Literacy-Project
It is a project for VITyarthi Digital Literacy project submission



                   OVERVIEW OF PROJECT

This project is based on Digital Literacy. This focuses on 5 tasks including creating Canva presentations, creating accounts in various portfolios, creating account in HackerRank and practising coding in that platform, then creating Google Forms and generating responses, writing professional emails to professors for extending assignment date and to internship company for summer internship programs, creating a social media Do’s and Don’ts checklist, making a checklist for preventing cyber crimes and creating a case study for awareness for cyber crimes in today’s digital world.  




Task– 01 
“Create a Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic.”

For this task, I used Canva to design my infographic because it is simple to use and has many ready-made templates. I selected a clean layout and created a presentation based on digital literacy for students. In my design, I included topics like what digital literacy means, some useful digital tools such as Google Docs, GitHub, and LinkedIn, basic safe internet practices, and a few points about email etiquette. 





Task -02 
“Build Your Student Digital Portfolio”

For building my professional digital presence, I created and updated profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle. GitHub is mainly used to showcase coding projects and track development work, so I plan to regularly upload my programming assignments and personal projects there. LinkedIn is a professional networking platform where I have added my education details, and I aim to use it to connect with seniors, professors, and industry professionals, as well as explore internship opportunities. 
Over the next four years, I intend to consistently update these platforms with my progress, projects, and achievements. This will help me build a strong portfolio that reflects my skills and learning journey. Initially, setting up profiles and understanding how to present information properly was a bit confusing, but it also helped me realize the importance of maintaining an online presence. Overall, this task gave me a clear starting point for developing my professional identity.

Task-03
“Explore Coding & Collaboration Platforms”

For this task, I used Google Forms to create a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz for my batchmates. I made a form with 5 questions, including multiple choice and short answer type. The questions were based on basic online safety, password security and general awareness about using internet. 
I also connected the form to Google Sheets to collect responses, which helped me to see all answers in one place. 
By doing this task, I learned how to create online forms and collect data in simple way. In future, this will help me in projects, surveys and group work. It also improved my digital skills and understanding of online tools which are important for students nowadays.




Task-04
“Draft a Professional Email & Etiquette Guide”

One situation where poor digital communication can create problem is when someone sends message without clear explanation. For example, a student sending message to teacher like “I will not submit” without proper reason can sound rude or careless. Because of this, teacher may think that student is not serious about studies.
This problem can be avoided if person think before sending message and use polite words. Writing full sentence with proper reason is important. Also rechecking the message before sending can help to avoid confusion. Good communication is very important in digital platforms for professional and positive environment.




Task-05
“Cybercrime Awareness Case Study & Prevention Guide”

While researching about cybercrime, what surprised me the most is how easily people can get scammed without even realising it. I used to think that only less educated people become victims, but actually even students and smart users also fall into these traps. The methods used by fraudsters are very convincing and sometimes look completely real, which makes it more dangerous.

One habit I will personally change is being more careful while using UPI and online links. Earlier, I did not pay much attention to small details, but now I will always double check before clicking any link or approving any payment request. I will also never share my OTP or personal details with anyone. This awareness is very important in today digital world.





                      CONCLUSION

I have learned a lot while doing all the 5 tasks in this Digital Literacy project. It helped me a lot to learn about the things happening around the digital platforms and it made me familiar with the websites and platforms that are very useful in doing a particular work. 
This project made me learn how to use Canva to make presentations, then I learned the ethics to write a professional email as a student, then learned some coding in the HackerRank, then I connected with many people on LinkedIn platform. After that I learned the cyber crime problems happening in today’s digital world, and the solutions to prevent any cyber crimes. 






This project also helped me understand that digital literacy is not only about using technology, but also about using it in a responsible and safe way. Earlier, I focused more on completing tasks using digital tools, but now I realize the importance of understanding how these platforms work and how they can impact my future.
I also understood that maintaining a digital identity is very important because whatever we do online can create an impression about us. Therefore, it is necessary to be careful about what we share, how we communicate, and how we present ourselves on platforms like LinkedIn and GitHub.
Another important learning from this project is that cyber safety should be a daily habit and not just something we think about occasionally. Small actions like using strong passwords, avoiding unknown links, and verifying information can make a big difference in preventing cyber crimes.
Overall, this project improved not only my technical skills but also my awareness, thinking, and responsibility as a digital user. It has prepared me to use digital platforms more effectively, safely, and professionally in my academic as well as future career.
